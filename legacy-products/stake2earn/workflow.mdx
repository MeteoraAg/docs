Stake2Earn has two connected workflows:

- A **creator workflow** that connects a DAMM v1 locked-liquidity pool to a Stake2Earn vault.
- A **staker workflow** where users stake the project token, compete for the leaderboard, earn released fees, claim quote rewards, and manage exits.

The product is designed to feel simple to users, but the mechanics matter: eligibility changes in real time, fee releases are dripped over time, and unstaking removes tokens from reward eligibility before withdrawal is complete.

## End-to-End Flow

```text
Create DAMM v1 pool + locked liquidity
        |
Initialize Stake2Earn vault
        |
Users stake the base token
        |
Top-staker leaderboard updates
        |
Vault collects locked-liquidity fees
        |
Fees drip to top stakers by stake share
        |
Users claim quote rewards; base rewards restake
        |
Users may request unstake, wait cooldown, then withdraw
```

## Creator Workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create or identify the DAMM v1 pool">
    Stake2Earn is designed for DAMM v1 constant-product pools. The pool pairs the project token, also called the stake token or base token, with a supported quote token such as SOL or USDC.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Lock liquidity for the pool">
    The pool's permanently locked liquidity is the reward source. Trading fees generated by that locked liquidity are claimable through the lock escrow and can be routed through the Stake2Earn vault.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Initialize the Stake2Earn vault">
    The creator configures the top-staker count, fee drip duration, unstake cooldown, and optional reward start time. These settings define who can earn, how quickly fees unlock, and how exits behave.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open staking to the community">
    Once the vault is live, holders can stake the project token. The vault tracks each user's staked amount and updates the top-staker list as balances change.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Maintain visibility around rewards">
    Stake2Earn works best when the community can see the leaderboard, total staked amount, eligible top-staker threshold, pending rewards, and claim history.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Staker Workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Stake the project token">
    A user deposits the project token into the Stake2Earn vault. The deposited amount becomes their staked balance.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Compete for leaderboard eligibility">
    The vault compares the user's staked balance against other stakers. If their balance is high enough to enter the configured top-staker list, they become eligible for future fee releases.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Earn released fees while eligible">
    When fees are released, the top-staker list shares them by stake weight. A larger stake inside the eligible group earns a larger share.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Claim quote rewards">
    Users can claim the quote-token portion of their rewards, such as SOL or USDC, up to the amount available for their account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Compound base-token rewards automatically">
    The project-token portion of rewards is not paid out directly. It is restaked into the user's position, increasing their staked balance and potentially strengthening their rank.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Unstake when ready">
    When a user requests an unstake, the requested amount stops counting as active stake and enters a cooldown. After the cooldown ends, the user can withdraw it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Leaderboard Mechanics

Stake2Earn uses a configurable top-staker list. The vault supports a top-staker count from **5 to 1,000** on production deployments.

A user can move in or out of the eligible list whenever stake balances change:

- If a user stakes enough to beat the lowest-ranked eligible staker, the user can enter the top list.
- If a top staker unstakes enough and another staker has a stronger balance, the top staker can be replaced.
- If two users have the same staked amount, earlier tracked positions are favored in ranking comparisons.
- If a user is outside the top list, they do not earn newly released fees until they become eligible.

<Info>
  Stake2Earn also tracks a broader full-balance list, up to 10,000 stakers in production, to help identify candidates who may enter the top-staker list when balances change.
</Info>

## Fee Collection and Release

Stake2Earn does not mint new rewards. It collects fees that have accrued to the locked DAMM v1 liquidity position.

Fees enter the reward system through two steps:

1. **Collection**: the vault claims available fees from the DAMM v1 lock escrow.
2. **Release**: collected fees are added to a locked fee balance and drip to eligible stakers over the configured fee drip duration.

The release model matters. A large fee collection does not have to become instantly claimable in one block. Instead, the product can distribute it gradually, giving the leaderboard a smoother reward curve.

<Note>
  Production deployments enforce a minimum interval between lock-escrow fee claims. This helps reduce precision loss from repeatedly claiming extremely small fee amounts.
</Note>

## Claim Experience

When a user claims, Stake2Earn first updates fee accounting for the vault and the user's stake account. Then it separates rewards into two product outcomes:

- **Quote-token rewards** are transferred to the user's quote-token account.
- **Base-token rewards** are treated as restaked rewards and added back into the user's staking position.

This design lets stakers realize value in the quote token while keeping project-token fee rewards aligned with the staking competition.

## Unstaking and Withdrawal

Stake2Earn separates **requesting an unstake** from **withdrawing tokens**.

### Request unstake

When a user requests an unstake:

- The unstaked amount is removed from their active staked balance.
- The amount stops counting toward leaderboard eligibility and reward share.
- An unstake record is created with a release time.
- The top-staker list may update immediately if the user's new balance changes their rank.

### Cancel unstake

If the user changes their mind during the cooldown, they can cancel the unstake. The canceled amount is returned to active stake and can count toward leaderboard eligibility again.

### Withdraw

After the cooldown ends, the user can withdraw the unstaked tokens from the vault.

<Warning>
  Tokens in the unstake cooldown are not active stake. They do not help the user keep a top-staker position while waiting for withdrawal.
</Warning>

## Operational Checklist

For a successful Stake2Earn deployment, teams should prepare:

- A DAMM v1 constant-product pool with the correct project token and quote token pairing.
- Permanently locked liquidity whose fees are intended to feed the Stake2Earn vault.
- A clear top-staker count that matches community size.
- A fee drip duration that balances smooth rewards with responsiveness.
- An unstake cooldown that is strict enough to discourage mercenary behavior but easy to explain.
- A leaderboard and claim interface that makes eligibility, pending rewards, and cooldowns visible.
- Clear user messaging that only top stakers earn newly released rewards.
