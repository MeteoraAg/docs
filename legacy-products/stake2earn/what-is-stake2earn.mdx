Stake2Earn is Meteora's legacy staking product for token teams that want to turn trading activity into holder alignment.

Instead of distributing a fixed emissions budget, Stake2Earn routes fees generated from permanently locked **DAMM v1** liquidity to the top stakers of the pool's base token. Holders stake the actual project token, compete for a ranked position, and earn a share of pool fees based on how much they stake relative to the other eligible top stakers.

The product goal is simple: make holding more active, make staking more competitive, and give communities a reason to keep participating after the pool is live.

## Why Stake2Earn Exists

Many token launches face the same post-launch problem: once trading opens, the strongest incentives often go to short-term traders instead of long-term community holders. Traditional farms can help, but they usually require separate reward budgets, emissions planning, and ongoing reward top-ups.

Stake2Earn uses a different source of rewards: trading fees from locked liquidity. When a DAMM v1 pool produces fees, the Stake2Earn vault can collect those fees and distribute them to the highest-ranked stakers. The more volume the pool generates, the more fee value can flow back into the staking competition.

<blockquote>
Stake2Earn changes the holder game from "who exits first?" to "who stakes enough to stay in the leaderboard?"
</blockquote>

## How Stake2Earn Works

Stake2Earn sits beside a DAMM v1 pool that already has permanently locked liquidity. The Stake2Earn vault owns the lock escrow for that liquidity, collects fee rewards from it, and then releases those rewards to eligible stakers over time.

```text
DAMM v1 locked liquidity generates trading fees
        |
Stake2Earn vault collects fees from the lock escrow
        |
Fees drip into the top-staker reward pool
        |
Top stakers accrue rewards by stake share
        |
Users claim quote rewards; base-token rewards compound into stake
```

### 1. A Creator Sets the Reward Arena

A creator or launchpad initializes a Stake2Earn vault for a supported DAMM v1 pool. The pool must pair the project token with a supported quote token, currently SOL or USDC, and the pool's locked liquidity escrow must be controlled by the Stake2Earn vault.

The creator chooses the product parameters that define the competition:

- **Top-staker count**: how many stakers are eligible for rewards.
- **Fee distribution start time**: when rewards begin flowing to stakers.
- **Fee drip duration**: how long newly collected fees take to fully release to stakers.
- **Unstake cooldown**: how long users must wait after requesting an unstake before they can withdraw.

### 2. Holders Stake the Project Token

Users stake the project's base token into the Stake2Earn vault. Their stake is tracked against all other stakers and used to determine whether they are inside the configured top-staker list.

If a user enters the top list, they begin earning from future fee releases. If they fall out of the top list, they stop earning new rewards from that point forward, while already accrued pending rewards remain tracked for their account.

### 3. Fees Are Collected and Dripped

Stake2Earn periodically collects fees from the DAMM v1 locked liquidity escrow. Those fees are not immediately dumped into a single instant distribution. They are added to a locked fee balance and released over the configured fee drip duration.

This makes rewards smoother and gives new fee collections a predictable distribution curve instead of a single winner-takes-now moment.

### 4. Top Stakers Earn Pro Rata

Only the current top-staker list is included in reward accounting. Within that eligible group, rewards are split by stake weight.

If the top-staker list has 100 eligible wallets and a user's stake represents 5% of the total stake inside that list, the user earns roughly 5% of the fees released during the period they were eligible, subject to integer rounding.

### 5. Users Claim and Compound

DAMM v1 pool fees can include both sides of the pair. Stake2Earn treats them differently for user experience and holder alignment:

- **Quote-token rewards** such as SOL or USDC are claimable by the user.
- **Project-token rewards** are automatically restaked, increasing the user's staked amount and potentially strengthening their leaderboard position.

This creates a flywheel: trading activity funds rewards, quote rewards give stakers tangible yield, and base-token rewards compound the staking competition instead of adding immediate sell pressure.

## What Makes Stake2Earn Different

Stake2Earn is not a normal farm, and it is not LP staking.

- **Not a fixed-emissions farm**: rewards depend on pool fee generation and collection, not a manually reloaded rewards campaign.
- **Not LP staking**: users stake the base token itself, not liquidity position tokens.
- **Not everyone earns at once**: eligibility is limited to the configured top-staker list.
- **Not instant-exit staking**: unstaked tokens must pass through a cooldown before withdrawal.
- **Not sell-pressure heavy by design**: base-token fee rewards are restaked instead of paid out as immediately claimable token rewards.

<Warning>
  Stake2Earn rewards depend on pool activity, fee collection, leaderboard position, and configured vault parameters. Being a token holder or staker does not guarantee rewards unless the wallet is inside the eligible top-staker set when fees are released.
</Warning>
