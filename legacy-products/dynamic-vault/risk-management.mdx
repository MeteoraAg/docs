Dynamic Vault is built to improve capital efficiency, but yield always comes with risk. The product manages risk through a combination of on-chain constraints, off-chain monitoring, strategy selection, allocation limits, and profit-unlocking mechanics.

The goal is not to claim that vault yield is risk-free. The goal is to make risks visible, bounded where possible, and actively monitored.

## Risk Categories

Dynamic Vault focuses on four main risk categories.

  <Card title="Operational Risk" icon="code" iconType="solid">

  </Card>
  <Card title="Lending Risk" icon="building-columns" iconType="solid">
    Risk that a connected lending market becomes too utilized or too illiquid for timely withdrawals.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Keeper Risk" icon="robot" iconType="solid">
    Risk that the off-chain operator is delayed, misconfigured, or compromised.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Timing Risk" icon="clock" iconType="solid">
    Risk that users attempt to exploit rebalance timing to capture profit unfairly.
  </Card>

## Operational Risk

Operational risk comes from the systems Dynamic Vault connects to and the operational controls around them. A lending protocol may upgrade its program, change behavior, hit an integration issue, or expose a bug.

Dynamic Vault reduces this risk with approved strategy management and allocation discipline.

### Strategy Approval

The vault can only deploy liquidity into strategies that are initialized and added to the vault. This prevents Hermes from routing funds into arbitrary destinations.

### Maximum Allocation Framework

Dynamic Vault documentation historically uses an allocation scoring framework to reduce exposure when a lending protocol does not meet certain criteria.

Criteria can include:

- whether the protocol has audits
- whether the code is open source
- whether an insurance fund exists
- whether the market is a main, liquid pool
- whether the integration has operated for enough time
- whether the program is controlled by multisig or is verified and non-upgradable

If a criterion is missing, the maximum allocation to that protocol can be reduced. The intent is simple: a newer, less transparent, or less battle-tested venue should not receive the same exposure as a more mature one.

<Note>
  Earlier Dynamic Vault documentation references a maximum allocation framework that starts from 100% and applies deductions across risk criteria, with an additional per-lending-platform cap. Specific parameters can be refined over time by Meteora governance and operators.
</Note>

## Lending Risk

Lending risk appears when supplied assets cannot be withdrawn quickly because borrowers are using most of the pool.

High utilization can be attractive because it often increases deposit APY. But high utilization also means there may be less reserve liquidity available for withdrawals.

Hermes monitors these conditions so the vault does not allocate based on APY alone.

## Keeper Risk

Hermes is powerful operational infrastructure, but it is not given unlimited authority.

The on-chain program constrains what the operator can do:

- operator actions must satisfy vault account constraints
- strategy deposits and withdrawals must use strategies tied to the vault
- reward claims go to treasury-controlled reward accounts
- principal cannot be sent to arbitrary external wallets through Hermes
- admins can transfer operator authority or disable vault deposits when needed

This means a compromised or malfunctioning keeper is limited to program-approved flows. It may cause bad allocation or missed opportunity, but it should not have unrestricted custody over vault principal.

## Timing Risk And Locked Profit

Dynamic Vault rebalances can realize profit. If that profit became withdrawable immediately, a user could try to enter right before a rebalance and leave right after it.

The locked profit mechanism reduces this risk.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Profit is reported during rebalance">
    The vault compares before and after liquidity and records the gain.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Profit is added to locked profit">
    Newly realized gain is not fully included in the withdrawable unlocked amount immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Locked profit unlocks over time">
    The vault gradually releases the profit into the unlocked amount used for deposits and withdrawals.
  </Step>
</Steps>

This makes rebalance profit harder to capture through short-term timing and better aligned with longer-term LP participation.

## User-Facing Risk Disclosures

Dynamic Vault users and integrators should understand the following.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Yield is variable">
    Returns depend on connected lending markets, utilization, borrowing demand, strategy availability, and rebalance timing.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Withdrawals depend on liquidity">
    The vault is designed for accessibility, but connected lending market reserves can affect withdrawal behavior during stressed conditions.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Strategies carry protocol risk">
    Liquidity deployed to lending strategies is exposed to the risks of those underlying protocols.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Keeper operations matter">
    Hermes monitoring and rebalance execution are part of the product's live operation. Delays or misconfiguration can affect allocation quality.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Warning>
  Dynamic Vault risk controls are designed to reduce and manage risk. They do not eliminate smart contract risk, lending market risk, liquidity risk, or operational risk.
</Warning>
