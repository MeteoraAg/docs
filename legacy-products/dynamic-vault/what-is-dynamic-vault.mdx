Dynamic Vault is Meteora's single-asset yield infrastructure for Solana. It lets users and protocols deposit one token, receive a vault LP token that represents their share of the vault, and have the underlying liquidity dynamically allocated across approved yield strategies.

The product was built around a simple idea: **idle liquidity should not stay idle**. Wallet balances, AMM reserves, protocol treasuries, and user deposits can all sit in the same asset vault and earn lending yield while remaining withdrawable.

<Info>
  Dynamic Vault runs on the mainnet program `24Uqj9JCLxUeoC3hGfh5W3s9FM9uCHDS2SG3LYwBpyTi`.
</Info>

## Why Dynamic Vault Exists

Most DeFi liquidity is productive only when it is actively deployed. A USDC balance sitting inside a wallet or protocol reserve is liquid, but it is not earning. A lending position can earn, but it creates operational overhead: integrations, monitoring, risk parameters, liquidity checks, APY comparisons, and rebalancing.

Dynamic Vault packages those responsibilities into a shared vault layer.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/overview/other-products/dynamic-vault/dynamic-vault.png" />
</Frame>

Instead of every application building its own lending allocator, applications can plug into Dynamic Vault. Users deposit into a vault for a specific token, Hermes monitors connected strategies, and the vault program records shares, strategy balances, fees, and profit unlocking on-chain.

## How Dynamic Vault Works

A Dynamic Vault has three core layers.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Users or protocols deposit a supported token">
    A depositor sends the underlying asset into the vault. The vault mints LP tokens based on the depositor's proportional share of the currently unlocked vault value.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The vault keeps liquid reserves and tracks strategies">
    The token vault holds available liquidity. Approved strategies hold deployed liquidity. The vault tracks the total amount across both places.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Hermes evaluates lending opportunities">
    Hermes monitors connected lending venues, available liquidity, APY, utilization, reserve depth, and risk thresholds.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Rebalance cranks move liquidity">
    When a rebalance is needed, Hermes submits a deposit or withdrawal strategy transaction. The vault updates total liquidity, strategy balances, profit, loss, and fees.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Users withdraw by burning LP tokens">
    Withdrawals burn vault LP tokens and return the user's share of the unlocked vault value. If the vault reserve does not hold enough liquidity, the program can withdraw directly from an approved strategy first.
  </Step>
</Steps>

```text
Deposit token -> Receive vault LP token -> Hermes allocates liquidity -> Yield accrues -> Withdraw by burning LP token
```

## Who Dynamic Vault Is For

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Liquidity providers">
    LPs can deposit a single asset into a vault and receive a share token that tracks their ownership of the vault's unlocked value.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="AMMs and trading products">
    Protocols can connect reserve liquidity to Dynamic Vault so assets can earn yield when they are not actively needed for swaps.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Wallets and frontends">
    Consumer apps can offer a simple yield experience without building lending allocation infrastructure from scratch.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Treasuries and ecosystem teams">
    Teams with idle single-token balances can route liquidity through a controlled vault layer instead of manually managing lending positions.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Warning>
  Dynamic Vault is a legacy product and depends on approved strategies, operator configuration, connected lending markets, and real-time liquidity conditions. Yield is not fixed, and risk controls reduce risk rather than remove it.
</Warning>
