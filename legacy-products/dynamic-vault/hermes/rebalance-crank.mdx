A rebalance crank is the transaction that changes where vault liquidity sits.

When Hermes decides that liquidity should move, it submits one of the vault's strategy actions:

- `deposit_strategy`: move liquidity from the vault reserve into an approved strategy
- `withdraw_strategy`: move liquidity from an approved strategy back into the vault reserve

The on-chain program then recalculates the vault's total liquidity and applies profit, loss, locked profit, and fee logic.

## What Happens During a Rebalance

<Steps>
  <Step title="Hermes selects an action">
    Hermes decides whether the vault should deposit into a strategy or withdraw from one based on yield and risk signals.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The vault checks permissions">
    The transaction must come from the admin or configured operator, and the strategy must match the vault's approved strategy records.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Liquidity moves through the strategy handler">
    The strategy handler performs the actual lending deposit or withdrawal through the connected protocol.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The vault compares before and after balances">
    The program measures liquidity in the vault reserve and strategy before and after the action.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Profit, loss, and fees are applied">
    Gains are added to locked profit and can trigger a performance fee. Losses reduce vault value and locked profit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Rebalance Accounting

Before the rebalance:

```math
beforeValue = tokenVaultBefore + strategyLiquidityBefore
```

After the rebalance:

```math
afterValue = tokenVaultAfter + strategyLiquidityAfter
```

The vault's total amount is updated by the difference.

```math
newTotalAmount = oldTotalAmount + afterValue - beforeValue
```

If the value increased, the rebalance created a gain. If the value decreased, the rebalance recorded a loss.

## Why Profit Is Locked

If all rebalance profit became withdrawable immediately, a user could attempt to deposit right before a known profitable rebalance and withdraw right after it. That user would capture yield that was earned by existing vault LPs.

Dynamic Vault reduces this timing attack by locking newly reported profit and releasing it gradually.

## How Profit Unlocking Works

When profit is realized, the vault adds it to `last_updated_locked_profit`. Over time, the locked amount degrades until it becomes fully available in the vault's unlocked value.

```math
unlockedAmount = totalAmount - lockedProfit
```

The unlocked amount is used for deposit and withdrawal share calculations.

## Product Benefit

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Protects Existing LPs" icon="shield" iconType="solid">
    Profit unlocking makes it harder for opportunistic deposits to capture yield immediately after a rebalance.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Keeps Accounting On-Chain" icon="calculator" iconType="solid">
    The vault program updates total amount, strategy liquidity, LP supply effects, and locked profit directly on-chain.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Allows Active Allocation" icon="arrows-rotate" iconType="solid">
    Hermes can still move liquidity as markets change without requiring users to manually manage lending positions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Constrains Operator Power" icon="lock" iconType="solid">
    Rebalance cranks can only interact with approved vault strategies and must satisfy program account constraints.
