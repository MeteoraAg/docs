Hermes is the keeper layer for Dynamic Vault. It is an off-chain service that watches lending markets, evaluates yield opportunities, monitors risk signals, and submits rebalance transactions to the on-chain vault program.

Hermes does not replace the vault program. It complements it.

- The **vault program** owns the accounting, LP token minting and burning, strategy allowlist, fee logic, and permission checks.
- **Hermes** handles market observation, allocation decisions, and operational timing.

This separation lets Dynamic Vault react to changing market conditions without giving an off-chain service unrestricted control over user funds.

<Note>
  Hermes can only move vault liquidity through approved strategy instructions. It cannot arbitrarily transfer principal to an external wallet.
</Note>

## Why Hermes Exists

Yield changes constantly. Lending APYs move as borrowers enter and exit. Utilization can spike. Deposit caps can be reached. A pool that was attractive an hour ago can become less attractive or less liquid.

Dynamic Vault needs an operator that can keep watching those conditions and act when the vault should rebalance. That operator is Hermes.

## What Hermes Monitors

Hermes evaluates several market and vault-level signals before submitting a rebalance.

## What Hermes Can Do

Hermes submits a limited set of operational actions.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Deposit into an approved strategy">
    If a lending strategy is attractive and safe within configured limits, Hermes can move vault reserve liquidity into that strategy.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Withdraw from an approved strategy">
    If a strategy becomes less attractive, too utilized, too illiquid, or needed for user withdrawals, Hermes can pull liquidity back into the vault reserve.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Claim supported strategy rewards">
    If a strategy emits rewards, Hermes can trigger reward collection to a treasury-controlled reward account.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What Hermes Cannot Do

Hermes is intentionally constrained.

- It cannot withdraw principal to an arbitrary wallet.
- It cannot route liquidity into strategies that the vault has not approved.
- It cannot bypass vault LP accounting.
- It cannot mint user LP shares outside the vault's deposit logic.
- It cannot ignore on-chain permission checks on admin, operator, strategy, reserve, and fee accounts.

## Hermes in the Dynamic Vault Stack

```text
Lending markets -> Hermes monitoring -> Allocation decision -> Rebalance crank -> Vault accounting update
```

Hermes makes Dynamic Vault feel dynamic. The on-chain vault protects the accounting. Together, they create a yield layer that can move with the market while keeping capital flows bounded by the program's rules.
