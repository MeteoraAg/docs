Dynamic Vault charges an operation fee when a rebalance produces profit. This is best understood as a performance fee: the vault only charges when strategy activity increases vault value.

<Info>
  The on-chain program constant charges a 5% performance fee on rebalance profit.
</Info>

## Why The Fee Is Paid In LP Tokens

The vault may not always have spare underlying tokens sitting idle in the reserve when profit is realized. Much of the vault's liquidity may be deployed into strategies.

Instead of forcing an underlying token transfer, the program mints additional vault LP tokens to the fee vault. This lets the fee vault own a proportional claim on vault value while keeping the underlying asset inside the vault system.

## Fee Flow

<Steps>
  <Step title="A rebalance reports profit">
    Hermes submits a rebalance crank. The vault compares before and after liquidity and detects a gain.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The vault calculates the fee">
    The operation fee is calculated as 5% of the profit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The vault mints LP tokens to the fee vault">
    Instead of sending underlying tokens out, the vault mints the correct LP token amount to represent the fee value.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Locked profit is adjusted">
    The fee-related unlocked portion is handled so share accounting stays consistent after fee minting.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Fee Formula

```math
fee = profit \times 5\%
```

The implementation calculates how many LP tokens should be minted to the fee vault using:

- `profit`: gain reported from the rebalance
- `unlockedAmount`: vault value currently available for share calculations
- `lpSupply`: LP token supply before the fee mint

At a product level:

```math
feeLpTokens = feeValue \times lpSupply / unlockedAmount
```

## Why This Design Matters

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="No Forced Reserve Drain" icon="water" iconType="solid">
    Fees do not require the vault to pull underlying tokens out of reserve liquidity at the moment of rebalance.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Aligned With Vault Shares" icon="receipt" iconType="solid">
    The fee vault receives LP tokens, meaning its fee claim rises and falls with the same vault share accounting as other LP tokens.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Only On Profit" icon="chart-line" iconType="solid">
    The fee is charged when the rebalance creates a gain, not simply because Hermes submitted an operation.
  </Card>
  <Card title="On-Chain Enforcement" icon="lock" iconType="solid">
    The fee logic is enforced by the vault program rather than being an off-chain accounting promise.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  The fee vault is owned by the treasury address. The fee claim is represented by vault LP tokens, not by an immediate transfer of the underlying deposited asset.
</Note>
