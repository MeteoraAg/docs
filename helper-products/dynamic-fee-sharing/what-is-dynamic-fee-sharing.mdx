Dynamic Fee Sharing is Meteora's on-chain fee distribution product. It lets a project route fees into a shared fee vault, define who should receive those fees, and let each recipient claim their portion directly on-chain.

Instead of collecting fees into one wallet and manually paying partners, creators, contributors, or communities later, Dynamic Fee Sharing turns the split into transparent vault logic. Fees enter the vault, the vault tracks each recipient's share, and recipients claim based on the share allocation configured at vault creation.

<Info>
  Dynamic Fee Sharing runs on the mainnet program `dfsdo2UqvwfN8DuUVrMRNfQe11VaiNoKcMqLHVvDPzh`.
</Info>

<Note>
  Dynamic Fee Sharing is not a trading fee model by itself. It is the distribution layer that can receive supported fee tokens and split them between multiple recipients.
</Note>

## Why Dynamic Fee Sharing Exists

Many on-chain products create revenue that needs to be shared: pool fees, rewards, launch fees, creator fees, partner fees, surplus, or ecosystem incentives. Without a dedicated distribution layer, teams usually have to choose between operational overhead and trust assumptions.

Dynamic Fee Sharing gives teams a cleaner model:

- Define the recipients once.
- Define each recipient's share once.
- Route fees into a shared vault.
- Let recipients claim their own allocation whenever fees are available.

## How Dynamic Fee Sharing Works

A Dynamic Fee Sharing setup has three core objects.

| Component | Product Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Fee vault | The main on-chain account that stores the recipient list, shares, funded fee accounting, and claim checkpoints. |
| Token vault | The token account that actually holds the fee token waiting to be claimed. |
| Recipients | The wallets that receive fees according to their configured shares. |

A typical lifecycle looks like this:

```text
Create fee vault
        |
Configure 2-5 recipients and shares
        |
Fund fees into the vault
        |
Recipients claim their share over time
```

## Fee Vault Types

Dynamic Fee Sharing supports two fee vault types.

### Non-PDA Fee Vault

A Non-PDA fee vault is created from an externally generated fee vault account. It is useful for standalone fee-sharing arrangements where the vault does not need to be tied to another on-chain entity.

Use this when:

- You want a simple standalone fee split.
- The vault is independent from a pool, launch, or position.
- You may want multiple vaults for the same token mint and owner.

### PDA Fee Vault

A PDA fee vault is deterministically derived from a base account and a token mint. It is useful when the fee split should be tied to another protocol object, such as a pool, launch, or fee-earning account.

Use this when:

- The vault should be discoverable from a base account and token mint.
- The vault is part of a product integration.
- The vault needs to claim fees from compatible source programs.

<Info>
  Both vault types use the same fee-sharing math after creation. The difference is how the vault address is created and whether it can act as a PDA signer for supported fee-claim integrations.
</Info>

## What Dynamic Fee Sharing Is Best For

Dynamic Fee Sharing is best when fees need to be split between a small, known group of recipients.

Good use cases include:

- Protocol fee sharing between a creator and launchpad.
- Pool fee sharing between strategy operators and owners.
- Reward sharing between partners or contributors.
- Campaign revenue splits.
- Treasury and contributor revenue distribution.
- On-chain payout transparency for recurring revenue streams.

<Warning>
  Recipient shares are configured when the fee vault is initialized. Teams should confirm recipients and share amounts before creating a vault, because the program does not provide a general recipient-edit flow.
</Warning>
