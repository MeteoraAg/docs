Alpha Vault is flexible by design. The same product can support a broad community allocation, a scarce first-come early access window, a curated whitelist, or a partner allocation.

This guide helps teams choose the right settings before opening deposits.

## Start With the Launch Goal

Before choosing parameters, decide what the vault is meant to optimize for.

| Launch Goal | Recommended Direction |
| --- | --- |
| Broad community participation | Pro Rata, permissionless or large whitelist, clear refund messaging. |
| Scarce early-access allocation | FCFS, hard cap, individual wallet caps. |
| Curated supporter list | Merkle proof whitelist or authority-managed access. |
| Bot-resistant public launch | Pro Rata with a clear deposit window and vesting schedule. |
| Small strategic allocation | Authority-managed whitelist with wallet-specific caps. |
| Strong holder alignment | Longer lock or vesting period after the vault buy. |

## Choose the Vault Mode

### Choose Pro Rata When Fairness Matters More Than Speed

Pro Rata is best when the team wants users to have time to join without turning the deposit window into a gas race.

Use Pro Rata when:

- Demand may exceed the vault's intended buying power.
- The project wants to include more wallets.
- Oversubscription is expected.
- Users should be able to withdraw before the deposit window closes.
- Refunds are acceptable and can be communicated clearly.

Product positioning: **"Everyone gets a proportional share of the vault result."**

### Choose FCFS When Scarcity and Simplicity Matter More

FCFS is best when the team wants a hard accepted-deposit cap and a simple message: deposits are accepted until the vault is full.

Use FCFS when:

- The project wants the vault to fill to a clear cap.
- The allocation is intentionally scarce.
- Users understand that speed matters.
- The project can use individual caps or whitelist caps to avoid concentration.
- The team does not want overflow refund mechanics.

Product positioning: **"Capacity is limited. Once the vault is full, deposits stop."**

## Choose the Right Cap

Alpha Vault uses different cap concepts depending on the mode.

| Cap | Applies To | Product Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Max buy cap | Pro Rata | Maximum quote amount the vault can use to buy from the pool. |
| Max deposit cap | FCFS | Maximum quote amount the vault can accept. |
| Individual cap | Mainly FCFS and whitelists | Maximum quote amount a wallet can deposit. |
| Whitelist cap | Whitelisted launches | Wallet-specific max cap for eligible users. |

A good cap should be large enough to make the vault meaningful, but not so large that the vault dominates the launch pool or creates unwanted sell pressure when tokens unlock.

## Decide Whether to Use a Whitelist

Use a whitelist if the vault is meant for a specific audience.

Whitelists are useful when allocation should be based on prior contribution, campaign participation, partner eligibility, NFT ownership, loyalty points, or other off-chain criteria.

Do not use a whitelist if the goal is a public community window and the team does not need to restrict access. In that case, permissionless Pro Rata is often easier for users to understand.

## Set the Deposit Window

The deposit window should be long enough for real users to see the announcement, understand the rules, and deposit without panic.

When choosing the window, consider:

- Time zones of the target community.
- How much education the launch needs.
- Whether users need time to bridge or prepare quote token.
- Whether a whitelist proof or UI eligibility check is involved.
- The required buffer between the deposit close and pool activation.

A rushed deposit window weakens the product benefit. If users feel forced to race, the launch starts to look like the public activation problem Alpha Vault is meant to improve.

## Design Vesting Around the Community Promise

Alpha Vault can release tokens immediately or gradually over time.

Shorter vesting is easier to understand and feels more liquid for participants. Longer vesting can create stronger alignment but may reduce user appetite if it feels too restrictive.

Use vesting when the team wants to:

- Reward long-term supporters.
- Reduce immediate post-launch sell pressure.
- Discourage purely extractive early buyers.
- Create a cleaner narrative around committed participation.

<Info>
  Vesting should match the launch story. If the vault is marketed as a supporter allocation, vesting can reinforce alignment. If it is marketed as a simple early-access sale, long vesting may need stronger explanation.
</Info>

## Plan Refund Communication

Refunds are a feature, not an afterthought.

Users should know whether refunds are possible before they deposit.

For Pro Rata launches, communicate:

- Deposits can exceed the max buy cap.
- Only part of each deposit may be used.
- Overflow quote can be refunded proportionally.
- Token allocation depends on the vault's final buy result.

For FCFS launches, communicate:

- The vault stops accepting deposits at the cap.
- Accepted deposits are intended to be used for buying.
- Users cannot withdraw after depositing.
- There is no overflow refund by design.

## Avoid Common Launch Mistakes

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Using FCFS without wallet caps">
    A single wallet can potentially fill the vault. If broad distribution matters, use individual caps, whitelist caps, or Pro Rata.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Opening deposits before whitelist data is ready">
    For Merkle proof launches, make sure eligibility data and proof hosting are ready before deposits open.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Making vesting too complicated">
    Users should understand when tokens unlock and how much they can claim. Simpler schedules are easier to market and support.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Not explaining refund timing">
    Users need to know when unused quote can be withdrawn and why the refundable amount may depend on total deposits and vault fills.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Setting caps without considering pool impact">
    The vault buy moves the launch pool price before public trading. Teams should choose caps that fit their desired launch dynamics.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Recommended Defaults by Launch Type

| Launch Type | Suggested Alpha Vault Design |
| --- | --- |
| Public community launch | Pro Rata, permissionless, clear max buy cap, simple vesting. |
| High-demand allowlist | Pro Rata, Merkle proof whitelist, wallet-specific caps. |
| Limited early-access round | FCFS, individual cap, short deposit window. |
| Partner or contributor allocation | Authority-managed whitelist, explicit wallet caps. |
| Long-term supporter campaign | Pro Rata or whitelist, vesting aligned with community expectations. |

## Launch Messaging Template

A clear announcement should answer these questions:

```text
Alpha Vault mode: Pro Rata or FCFS
Accepted token: SOL / USDC / other quote token
Access: Permissionless or whitelisted
Deposit window: Start and end time
Vault cap: Max buy cap or max deposit cap
Wallet cap: If applicable
Withdrawal rule: Whether deposits can be withdrawn before close
Claim schedule: Immediate, locked, or vesting
Refund rule: When unused quote can be claimed
Connected pool: DLMM, DAMM v1, or DAMM v2
```

If users can explain the vault in one sentence after reading the announcement, the launch is in good shape.
