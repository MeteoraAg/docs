Alpha Vault is Meteora's launch helper product for teams that want to give genuine supporters a structured way to buy into a token before the pool opens for public trading.

It works alongside a Meteora Launch Pool. Before public trading starts, users deposit a quote token such as SOL or USDC into the Alpha Vault. During the pool's pre-activation window, the vault is whitelisted to buy the launch token from the pool before everyone else can trade. After the vault has purchased tokens, users receive their allocation according to the vault mode and claiming schedule.

<Info>
  Alpha Vault is available for **DLMM**, **DAMM v1**, and **DAMM v2** launch pools.
</Info>

<Note>
  Alpha Vault is not a separate trading venue. It is a controlled early-access layer that sits in front of a Meteora Launch Pool.
</Note>

## Why Alpha Vault Exists

Token launches often face the same problem: the first few seconds of public trading can be dominated by sniper bots, priority-fee wars, and wallets that only want the earliest entry before real supporters can participate.

Alpha Vault gives teams a cleaner launch path. Instead of forcing supporters to compete at pool activation, the project can open a deposit window before launch, collect demand into one vault, let that vault buy during the protected pre-activation period, and then distribute the bought tokens back to participants under transparent rules.

## How Alpha Vault Works

An Alpha Vault launch has four product stages.

```text
Create Launch Pool + Alpha Vault
        |
Open deposit window
        |
Vault buys during pre-activation
        |
Users claim tokens and refunds by vault rules
```

### 1. Create the Launch Pool and Alpha Vault

The project creates a Meteora Launch Pool and attaches an Alpha Vault to it. The vault is configured before launch so the pool knows which vault is allowed to buy during the protected pre-activation window.

The project chooses settings such as:

- The quote token accepted by the vault, usually SOL or USDC.
- The pool type: DLMM, DAMM v1, or DAMM v2.
- The Alpha Vault mode: Pro Rata or FCFS.
- The maximum amount the vault can buy or accept.
- Whether deposits are permissionless or whitelisted.
- Any individual wallet cap.
- When deposits open and close.
- When purchased tokens start vesting and when vesting ends.

### 2. Users Deposit Before Launch

During the deposit window, users deposit the quote token into the vault. The vault does not start with launch tokens. It starts by collecting quote assets from users who want early exposure.

Depending on the vault mode, deposits can behave differently:

- **Pro Rata** can accept deposits above the max buy cap, then allocate the filled amount proportionally.
- **FCFS** stops accepting deposits once the deposit cap is filled.

The project can also decide whether the vault is open to everyone or restricted to a whitelist.

### 3. Vault Buys Before Public Trading

After deposits close, the Launch Pool enters its pre-activation buying period. During this period, the Alpha Vault can buy from the pool before normal public trading opens.

Product-wise, this is the key anti-sniper moment. Instead of every supporter racing bots at public activation, the vault executes as a single launch mechanism during the protected window. Quote tokens move from the vault into the pool, and purchased launch tokens move from the pool into the vault.

The vault can buy up to its allowed amount. If the vault cannot use every deposited quote token, unused quote can be returned according to the refund rules.

### 4. Users Claim Tokens and Refunds

After the vault has bought tokens, users claim their share from the vault. Claims can be available immediately at the configured start point or released gradually through vesting.

Users may also claim unused quote tokens when applicable, such as:

- Overflow deposits in Pro Rata mode when total deposits exceed the vault's max buy cap.
- Remaining quote if the vault did not spend all swappable quote during the buying period.

## Past Alpha Vault Launches

Projects that have used Alpha Vault for token launches include **UpRock** (UPT), **Sanctum** (CLOUD), **dVin Labs** (VIN), **deBridge** (DBR), **Streamflow** (STREAM), **SonicVM** (SONIC), and **Sendcoin** (SEND).

Read the CLOUD launch case study [here](https://meteoraag.medium.com/dlmm-case-study-the-cloud-launch-08eaa5ab3b9c).

<Warning>
  Alpha Vault is highly configurable. The right mode, cap, whitelist, and vesting schedule depends on the launch's goals. Teams should test their configuration and communicate the rules clearly before opening deposits.
</Warning>
