description: The main product decisions creators and launchpads should make before creating a Presale Vault.

A strong Presale Vault launch starts before the vault is created. This page helps teams think through the key product settings: sale mode, caps, buckets, whitelist design, fees, vesting, and unsold token handling.

# 1. Choose the sale objective

Start with the launch outcome you want.

## Price certainty

If you want buyers to know the exact token price before depositing, use **Fixed Price**.

Best for:

- clear valuation messaging
- strategic or community sales
- launches where buyers expect a simple token-per-dollar calculation

## Urgency and hard-cap demand

If you want a capped sale that fills as buyers arrive, use **FCFS**.

Best for:

- high-energy campaigns
- simple public sales
- launches where scarcity is part of the experience

## Fair allocation under high demand

If you expect demand to exceed supply and want eligible buyers to have time to participate, use **Pro Rata**.

Best for:

- community-first launches
- heavily anticipated tokens
- allowlisted rounds where fairness matters

# 2. Select the quote token

The quote token is what buyers deposit.

Common choices:

- SOL for native Solana audiences
- USDC or USDT for stable unit-of-account fundraising
- a partner or ecosystem token for community-specific sales

A stable quote token makes marketing and cap communication easier. A volatile quote token can add market risk between deposit, completion, and liquidity deployment.

# 3. Set the raise caps

Every sale needs a minimum and maximum cap.

## Minimum cap

The minimum cap is the amount needed for the sale to succeed. If the sale raises less than this amount, buyers can withdraw their deposits.

Use the minimum cap to answer: "What is the smallest raise that still makes this launch worth completing?"

## Maximum cap

The maximum cap is the target raise ceiling.

Use the maximum cap to answer: "How much capital do we want to accept before the sale is considered full?"

## Practical guidance

- Set the minimum cap low enough that the sale can realistically succeed.
- Set the maximum cap based on token supply, launch valuation, and post-sale liquidity plans.
- For Pro Rata, remember that deposits can exceed the maximum cap, but unused quote tokens are refundable.
- For Fixed Price and FCFS, reaching the maximum cap can end the sale early unless disabled.

# 4. Allocate presale supply

The presale supply is the base token amount available to buyers.

When choosing presale supply, consider:

- launch valuation
- circulating supply at token generation
- expected community size
- vesting and lock schedule
- liquidity deployment after the sale
- partner, team, treasury, or ecosystem allocations outside the presale

In Fixed Price mode, the price determines how much of the allocated supply gets sold. In FCFS and Pro Rata modes, the allocated supply for a participating bucket is distributed proportionally by deposit share.

# 5. Decide whether to use buckets

Buckets let you create separate lanes inside the same Presale Vault.

Each bucket has its own:

- token allocation
- buyer minimum deposit
- buyer maximum deposit
- deposit fee

Use buckets when different audiences need different sale terms.

# 6. Set buyer caps

Buyer caps shape distribution.

## Minimum deposit

The minimum deposit prevents dust participation and keeps the sale meaningful for each buyer.

Use a higher minimum when:

- the sale targets serious participants
- buyer count is expected to be manageable
- the claim and vesting experience should avoid tiny allocations

Use a lower minimum when:

- broad community access matters
- the token has a large retail audience
- the project wants many smaller holders

## Maximum deposit

The maximum deposit limits concentration.

Use a lower maximum when:

- fairness and decentralization matter
- the launch has strong demand
- whales could dominate allocation

Use a higher maximum when:

- the sale targets fewer strategic buyers
- the project needs larger commitments
- the audience has already been curated through a whitelist

# 7. Choose the access model

## Permissionless

Anyone can participate.

Best for:

- public launches
- simple campaigns
- broad discovery

Tradeoff: less control over who gets allocation.

## Permissioned with authority

The creator or approved operator controls escrow creation for buyers.

Best for:

- smaller curated lists
- partner rounds
- private sales
- cases where the team wants operational control

Tradeoff: more coordination from the creator or launchpad.

## Permissioned with Merkle proof

Buyers prove they are allowlisted using a Merkle proof.

Best for:

- large whitelists
- token-gated communities
- NFT holder lists
- campaigns where thousands of eligible wallets need scalable access

Tradeoff: the allowlist and proof distribution must be prepared before deposits open.

# 8. Decide on deposit fees

Deposit fees can be configured per bucket.

This is useful for launchpads that want to monetize sale infrastructure or creators that want different fee treatment across participant groups.

Examples:

- 0 bps for early supporters
- 100 bps for a partner platform
- 500 bps for a public sale bucket
- different fees for different launchpad tiers

<Info>
  Deposit fees are expressed in basis points and can be configured up to 5,000 bps, or 50%.
</Info>

# 9. Design the unlock schedule

Unlock design is one of the most important product decisions. It affects buyer expectations, post-launch sell pressure, and community alignment.

## 100% immediate release

Best for:

- simple public sales
- tokens designed for immediate trading
- launches where buyers expect full liquidity

Tradeoff: higher potential sell pressure after claims open.

## Partial immediate release with vesting

Best for:

- rewarding early buyers while reducing instant supply shock
- serious community rounds
- launches where long-term alignment matters

Example: 20% claimable at launch, 80% vested over 180 days.

## Full lock then vesting

Best for:

- strategic rounds
- partner allocations
- campaigns where buyers are expected to commit long term

Example: 0% immediate release, 30-day lock, then 180-day linear vesting.

# 10. Choose the unsold token action

If the sale does not sell all allocated base tokens, the creator chooses what happens to the unsold amount.

## Refund to creator

Use this when unsold tokens should return to treasury, future incentives, liquidity, or another allocation plan.

## Burn

Use this when the project wants the sale outcome to reduce supply if demand is lower than expected.

Burning can be easier to message as a supply-discipline decision, but it should be consistent with the project's tokenomics.

# 11. Plan post-sale operations

Presale Vault does not automatically deploy liquidity. After the sale completes, the creator should be ready to:

- withdraw raised quote tokens
- collect deposit fees, if any
- handle unsold base tokens
- deploy liquidity to the intended Meteora pool or launch venue
- communicate claim timing and vesting details to buyers
- provide instructions for refunds if the sale was Pro Rata or failed

# Recommended launch templates

## Fair community launch

- Mode: Pro Rata
- Access: Merkle-proof whitelist
- Buckets: community bucket plus optional partner bucket
- Buyer cap: moderate
- Unlock: partial immediate release with vesting
- Unsold tokens: refund to creator or burn depending on tokenomics

## Fast public sale

- Mode: FCFS
- Access: permissionless or whitelist
- Buckets: one public bucket
- Buyer cap: strict enough to prevent concentration
- Unlock: immediate or short vesting
- Unsold tokens: refund to creator

## Strategic presale

- Mode: Fixed Price
- Access: permissioned with authority
- Buckets: partner or VIP buckets
- Buyer cap: higher, based on negotiated allocation
- Unlock: lock plus linear vesting
- Unsold tokens: refund to creator

# Pre-launch QA checklist

Before deposits open, confirm:

- the quote token and base token are correct
- the sale start and end times are correct
- the minimum and maximum caps match the launch plan
- the selected mode matches the campaign's allocation strategy
- bucket supplies add up to the intended presale allocation
- buyer caps are appropriate for each bucket
- whitelist data is ready, if using a permissioned sale
- deposit fees are intentional and clearly communicated
- unlock and vesting dates are communicated to buyers
- unsold token handling is aligned with tokenomics
- the team has a plan for raised capital and liquidity deployment
