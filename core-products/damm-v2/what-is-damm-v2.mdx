DAMM v2 is Meteora's highly configurable Dynamic AMM for Solana markets. It is built for teams that need more than a simple pool: launch teams can tune fees, open trading at a specific moment, choose how liquidity behaves, support modern token standards, and give LPs transferable NFT positions in one product.

At its core, DAMM v2 uses constant-product market making. That means every pool balances two token reserves so traders can swap against shared liquidity. DAMM v2 expands that familiar model with product controls for launch protection, capital efficiency, fee collection, auto-compounding, liquidity mining, and long-term liquidity commitments.

## Why DAMM v2 Exists

Token markets need different liquidity behavior at different stages. A new launch may need elevated anti-sniper fees and quote-token fee collection. A mature pair may need deeper full-range liquidity and auto-compounding. A launchpad may need repeatable pool settings across many launches. A project treasury may need permanently locked liquidity that still earns fees and incentives.

DAMM v2 is designed to support those different needs without forcing every pool into the same template.

## How DAMM v2 Works

A DAMM v2 pool is created around two tokens: token A and token B. Token B is commonly the quote asset, such as SOL or USDC. The pool has an initial price, liquidity, a fee configuration, and an activation setting that determines when public trading begins.

Pool creators can use static or dynamic configurations:

- **Static configurations** reuse pre-defined settings, useful for launchpads and repeatable pool templates.
- **Dynamic configurations** allow pool-specific choices, useful when each market needs different fees, ranges, or launch controls.
- **Customizable pools** expose the highest level of configuration for specialized integrations.

For LPs, the experience is position-based. When you add liquidity, you hold a position NFT. That position tracks your liquidity, your pending fees, your pending liquidity mining rewards, and any liquidity that is locked or vesting.

## Product Pillars

### Concentrated Liquidity

DAMM v2 can create pools with a fixed minimum and maximum price range. Liquidity inside that range is used more efficiently than full-range liquidity because capital is focused where trading is expected to happen.

This is useful for launch teams that want tighter market depth around an expected price area, or for integrators that want predictable pool boundaries.

### Compounding Liquidity

DAMM v2 also supports a compounding mode where the pool behaves like a full-range constant-product AMM. A configurable share of LP fees is automatically added back into pool reserves, increasing depth over time instead of requiring every fee to be manually claimed.

This is useful for long-lived pools where LPs want passive liquidity growth and less operational overhead.

### Flexible Fees

DAMM v2 separates fee behavior into layers:

- A **base fee mode** defines the pool's primary trading fee behavior.
- An optional **dynamic fee** raises fees when volatility increases.
- A **protocol fee** can be taken from the trading fee.
- In compounding mode, a share of the LP fee can be reinvested into pool reserves.

The five base fee modes are time scheduler linear, time scheduler exponential, market cap scheduler linear, market cap scheduler exponential, and rate limiter.

### NFT Positions

Every DAMM v2 position is backed by a unique position NFT. The NFT is the ownership handle for the position, so transferring the NFT transfers control of the liquidity position.

Positions can also be split, which makes it easier to separate liquidity tranches, distribute ownership, or transfer part of a position without closing the whole thing.

### Liquidity Mining

DAMM v2 includes liquidity mining in the pool program itself. A pool can support up to two reward tokens, and rewards accrue to positions based on their share of total liquidity.

This gives projects a direct way to incentivize LPs without deploying and managing a separate staking contract.
