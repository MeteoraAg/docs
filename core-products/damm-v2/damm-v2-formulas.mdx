DAMM v2 is designed around constant-product liquidity. The formulas below explain the ideas behind the product without requiring you to integrate directly with the on-chain math.

## Constant Product

The basic AMM idea is simple:

```math
x \times y = k
```

`x` is the token A reserve, `y` is the token B reserve, and `k` is the pool's liquidity invariant. When a trader adds one token to the pool and removes the other, the pool adjusts the price so the relationship between both reserves stays balanced.

For users, this means:

- Larger pools usually create lower price impact.
- Larger trades move price more than smaller trades.
- Pool depth matters most around the prices where trading happens.

## Price and Square-Root Price

DAMM v2 stores price as square-root price:

```math
\sqrt{P} = \sqrt{\frac{\text{token B}}{\text{token A}}}
```

This format makes liquidity math more precise and efficient on-chain. In product terms, you can think of it as the pool's current price, expressed in a way that makes adding, removing, and swapping liquidity easier for the program to calculate.

## Concentrated Liquidity Range

In concentrated liquidity mode, a DAMM v2 pool has a fixed lower and upper price:

```math
\sqrt{P_{\min}} \le \sqrt{P} \le \sqrt{P_{\max}}
```

Liquidity is active inside that range. If the market moves outside the configured range, the pool reaches its boundary and swaps cannot continue through that range.

The practical product tradeoff is:

- A tighter range can make liquidity more efficient near the target price.
- A wider range can support more price movement.
- Full-range liquidity behaves more like a traditional constant-product pool.

## Token Amounts for Liquidity

When an LP adds liquidity to a concentrated pool, DAMM v2 calculates how much token A and token B are needed based on the current price and the pool's range.

For a liquidity amount `L`:

```math
\Delta A = L \times \left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{P}} - \frac{1}{\sqrt{P_{\max}}}\right)
```

```math
\Delta B = L \times (\sqrt{P} - \sqrt{P_{\min}})
```

In plain English:

- Token A is more important as price approaches the lower side of the range.
- Token B is more important as price approaches the upper side of the range.
- The current pool price determines the deposit ratio.

<Note>
  This is why DAMM v2 may ask for different token ratios depending on the current price, even when the pool range stays the same.
</Note>

## Compounding Liquidity

In compounding mode, DAMM v2 uses full-range constant-product liquidity. Instead of tracking custom lower and upper price bounds, the pool tracks token reserves directly:

```math
\text{token A reserve} \times \text{token B reserve} = k
```

The pool price comes from the reserve ratio:

```math
P = \frac{\text{token B reserve}}{\text{token A reserve}}
```

A share of LP fees can be added back into reserves. As reserves grow, the pool gets deeper, and every LP benefits in proportion to their position liquidity.

## Trading Fees

DAMM v2 trading fees are built in layers:

```math
\text{Total Trading Fee} = \text{Base Fee} + \text{Dynamic Fee}
```

The base fee is chosen from one of DAMM v2's five base fee modes. The dynamic fee is optional and increases when recent price movement is volatile.

After the total trading fee is calculated, DAMM v2 splits it:

```math
\text{Protocol Fee} = \text{Trading Fee} \times \text{Protocol Fee Percent}
```

```math
\text{LP Fee} = \text{Trading Fee} - \text{Protocol Fee}
```

If the pool uses compounding mode, the LP fee can be split again:

```math
\text{Compounding Fee} = \text{LP Fee} \times \frac{\text{Compounding Fee Bps}}{10{,}000}
```

```math
\text{Claimable Fee} = \text{LP Fee} - \text{Compounding Fee}
```

For non-compounding pools, the compounding fee is zero and LP fees remain claimable by positions.

## Dynamic Fee

Dynamic fees use a volatility accumulator. When price changes quickly, the accumulator rises. When the market calms down, it decays.

```math
\text{Dynamic Fee} \propto (\text{Volatility Accumulator} \times \text{Bin Step})^2
```

For product users, the important idea is not the exact coefficient. It is that DAMM v2 can charge more during volatile periods and less during quiet periods, creating a fee response that adapts to market conditions.

## Liquidity Mining Rewards

DAMM v2 liquidity mining distributes reward tokens by position liquidity:

```math
\text{Reward Per Liquidity} = \frac{\text{Elapsed Time} \times \text{Reward Rate}}{\text{Total Pool Liquidity}}
```

```math
\text{Position Reward} = \text{Position Liquidity} \times \text{Reward Per Liquidity Since Last Checkpoint}
```

This means LPs earn rewards in proportion to their share of pool liquidity. Unlocked, vesting, and permanently locked liquidity can all count toward rewards because the position's total liquidity is used for reward accounting.
